§ 19-103. MEETINGS OF THE BOARD (§ 19-103)

103.1 103.1 The Chairperson of the Board shall preside at all meetings of the Boardt which he or she is present, and shall perform such other duties as may beequired of him or her by the Board.

103.2 103.2 The Vice Chairperson of the Board shall, in the absence of thehairperson, preside at its meetings and shall perform such other duties as maye required of him or her by the Board.

103.3 103.3 Regular meetings of the Board shall be held no less than once every sixty60) days at such time and place as the Chairperson shall determine. At leasthree (3) business days in advance of each regular meeting of the Board, noticehall be given to each Member and to the public. However, seven (7) businessays’ notice shall be given for regular meetings if, in the opinion of thehairperson, the matters to be voted upon by the Board at such meeting couldotentially have an adverse impact on the community.

103.4 103.4 Special meetings of the Board may be called at the discretion of thehairperson or at the request of any six (6) Members. At least forty-eight (48)ours in advance of each special meeting of the Board, notice shall be given toach Member and to the public.

103.5 103.5 At least seven (7) calendar days before each meeting of the Boardspecial or regular) at which amendments to the bylaws are to be considered,otice shall be given to each Member and to the public.

103.6 103.6 Notice of a meeting of the Board shall specify the date, time and placef the meeting.

103.7 103.7 Notice must be either delivered personally to each Member, or mailed viahe United States Postal Service (USPS), facsimile transmission or electronicail to his or her business address. If such notice is given by USPS, it shalle deemed delivered when deposited in the United States mail properly addressednd with postage prepaid thereon. If such notice is sent by telecopy, electronicail or delivered personally, it shall be deemed delivered when received.owever, a Member may waive notice of any regular or special meeting by writtentatement filed with the Board. Attendance at a meeting shall also constitute aaiver of notice.

103.8 103.8 Public notice shall be given by publication in the D.C. Register or in aewspaper of general circulation.
