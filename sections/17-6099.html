§ 17-6099. DEFINITIONS (§ 17-6099)

6099.1. For purposes of this chapter, the following terms have the meanings ascribed:

Act &#8211; Health Occupation Revision Act of 1985 (&#8220;Act&#8221;), effective March 25, 1986 (D.C. Law 6-99; D.C. Official Code $8 3-1201.01 et seq.).

Applicant &#8211; a person applying for a license to practice as a clinical nurse specialist under this chapter.

Board &#8211; the Board of Nursing, established by § 204 of the Act, D.C. Official Code § 3-1202.04.

Certificate &#8211; a certificate to practice as a clinical nurse specialist under this chapter.

Clinical practice &#8211; the routine application of the principles of clinical nurse specialist to the diagnosis and treatment of disease and the maintenance of health.

Graduate &#8211; an individual who has graduated from a post-basic nursing education program for preparation to practice as a clinical nurse specialist.

Nationally recognized certifying body &#8211; is one that offers a national certification examination in the specialty area of advanced nursing practice, utilizes standards and principles of the American Board of Nursing Specialties (ABNS), and provides a mechanism for evaluating continued competency in the speciality area of nursing practice.

Supervisor &#8211; means a certified clinical nurse specialist who is responsible for the supervision of a student, graduate, or applicant.

6099.2. The definitions in § 4099 of chapter 40 of this title are incorporated by reference into and are applicable to this chapter.
