§ 17-7809. CONTINUING EDUCATION PROGRAMS AND ACTIVITIES (§ 17-7809)

7809.1 7809.1 A continuing education hour shall be valid only if it is part of arogram or activity approved by the Board.

7809.2 7809.2 To qualify for approval by the Board, a continuing education programhall:

(a) Be current in its subject matter;

(b) Be developed and taught by qualified individuals;

(c) Be submitted by the program provider to the Board for review no less thanhirty (30) days prior to the date of the presentation and be approved by theoard; and

(d) Have been completed during the period for which credit is claimed andnclude documentation of the following:

A copy of the official program or syllabus;

The presentation title;

(3) The date of the presentation;

(4) The contact hours or credits awarded for the presentation; and

(5) The type of audience for which the program is intended.

7809.3 7809.3 The Board shall accept for credit programs or activities sponsored byhe following organizations (provided that the applicant submits verification ofttendance):

(a) The District of Columbia Speech-Language-Hearing Association or similarpeech-language hearing association of another state;

(b) The American Academy of Audiology;

(c) The American Speech-Language-Hearing Association (ASHA) or its approvedontinuing education providers;

(d) An accredited provider of The Accreditation Council on Continuing Medicalducation of the American Medical Association offering Category I continuingedical education;

(e) The International Association of Continuing Education and Training (IACET)r its authorized providers; or

(f) A health care organization accredited by the Joint Commission onccreditation of Healthcare Organizations (JCAHO).

7809.4 7809.4 The Board may accept for credit the following activities:

(a) A course given at an accredited college or university;

(b) A seminar or workshop;

(c) An educational program given at a conference;

(d) In-service training;

(e) Serving as a presenter or speaker at a conference, seminar, workshop, orn-service training; or

(f) Publishing an article in a professional journal, a book or a chapter in aook, or a book review in a professional journal related to audiology orpeech-language pathology.

7809.5 7809.5 The Board may, in its discretion, approve additional continuingducation programs and activities that contribute to the growth of an applicantn professional competence in the practice of audiology or speech-languageathology and which meet the other requirements of this section.

7809.6 7809.6 The Board shall not grant credit for work done in the course of anpplicant’s normal occupation or incident to the performance of his or heregular duties, such as teaching courses, research, or course preparation in thease of a teacher or professor.

7809.7 7809.7 An applicant shall have the burden of verifying whether a program ispproved by the Board.

7809.8 7809.8 An applicant shall prove completion of required continuing educationours by submitting upon request the following information with respect to eachrogram:

(a) The name and address of the provider of the program;

(b) The name of the program, its location, a description of the subject matterovered, and the names of the instructors;

(c) The dates on which the applicant attended the program;

(d) The hours of credit claimed; and

(e) Verification by the course provider or accreditor of completion, byignature, stamp, or official transcript in the case of accreditors.
