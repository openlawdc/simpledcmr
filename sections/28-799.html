§ 28-799. DEFINITIONS (§ 28-799)

799.1 799.1 When used in this chapter, the following terms and phrases shall have theeanings prescribed:

Absconder &#8211; A pretrial CCC defendant conditionally released pursuant to.C. Official Code § 23-1321(c)(1)(B)(xi) (2001) who fails to return to his/herssigned CCC after an authorized release into the community or who leaves theCC without authorization. The failure to return after an authorized release orn unauthorized absence is a violation of the conditions of release and mayesult in a prosecution for contempt of court pursuant to the provisions of D.C.fficial Code § 23-1329, or escape under D.C. Official Code § 22-2601.

Administrative hold &#8211; Confinement of a CCC defendant to the center, for aeriod not to exceed twenty-four (24) hours (excluding weekends and holidays),ending the investigation of a complaint/allegation of a program or centeriolation.

Altered item &#8211; An item that has been modified or remanufactured forurposes other than the original use.

Chain of custody &#8211; The practice of preserving evidence and documenting theecord of who handled and controlled the evidence.

Contraband &#8211; Any unauthorized item found in the possession or control of aCC defendant.

Curfew &#8211; The designated time that a CCC defendant is required by courtrder or program requirement to return to the CCC following an authorizedelease to the community.

Curfew violator &#8211; A CCC defendant who fails to return to the CCC at theuthorized time, returns after curfew, but before a warrant for escape isssued.

Detention &#8211; The placement of a CCC defendant in the Central Detentionacility (CDF) following a formal complaint/allegation of a Class I Infractionending judicial intervention and/or review, or following a formalomplaint/allegation of a Class II Infraction by a defendant who is reasonablyelieved to present a high risk of danger to self or others, or presents a riskf flight, and who has been remanded to the CDF for more than twenty-four (24)ours.

Disciplinary team &#8211; The CCC staff designated by the CCCdministrator/Director to conduct disciplinary hearings for violations of ClassI or Class III infractions.

Evidence &#8211; Any item or information that tends to establish or disprove aact.

Room Confinement/Restriction &#8211; The confinement of a CCC defendant, wheneemed appropriate by the CCC Administrator/Director or designee, to a roomithin the facility except to attend work, school, scheduled medicalppointments, meals, legal visits, training programs and community service.

SOURCE
