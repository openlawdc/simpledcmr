§ 29-6899. DEFINITIONS (§ 29-6899)

6899.1. The following terms and phrases shall have the meanings ascribed:

&#8220;Adult&#8221; &#8211; An individual who is 18 years of age or older.

&#8220;Applicant&#8221; &#8211; An individual who is applying for a subsidy provided under the Grandparent Caregivers Pilot Program.

&#8220;Application&#8221; &#8211; A written request to be found eligible for a subsidy submitted pursuant to this chapter.

&#8220;CFSA&#8221; -The District of Columbia Child and Family Services Agency.

&#8220;Child&#8221; &#8211; An individual who is under eighteen (18) years of age and who is or prior to being adopted by applicant was the grandchild, grand-nephew or grand-niece, as appropriate, of the applicant or recipient.

&#8220;Court&#8221; &#8211; Any court of competent jurisdiction.

&#8220;Crime against a child&#8221; &#8211; A criminal offense which is comparable to:

(a) Kidnapping of a child, except by a parent;

(b) False imprisonment of a child, except by a parent;

(c) Criminal sexual conduct toward a child;

(d) Solicitation of a child to engage in sexual conduct;

(e) Use of a child in a sexual performance;

(f) Solicitation of a child to practice prostitution;

(g) Any conduct that by its nature is a sexual offense against a child; or

(h) Production or distribution of child pornography.

&#8220;Crime of violence&#8221; &#8211; Has the meaning set forth in D.C. Official Code § 23-1331 (2005 Supp).

&#8220;Criminal background check&#8221; &#8211; The investigation of an individual&#8217;s criminal history through the record systems of both the Federal Bureau of Investigation and the Metropolitan Police Department.

&#8220;DCMR&#8221; &#8211; The District of Columbia Municipal Regulations.

&#8220;Household income&#8221; &#8211; The combined gross income of the applicant or recipient, as appropriate, and all other individuals, including children, residing in the applicant or recipient&#8217;s home.

&#8220;Legal custodian&#8221; &#8211; The person to whom a court of competent jurisdiction has awarded custody.

&#8220;Legal custody&#8221; &#8211; A legal status awarded by a court which vests in a custodian the responsibility for the custody of a minor and that includes rights and responsibilities that are substantially similar to those set out in D.C. Official Code § 16-2301(21) (2005 Supp).

&#8220;Minor&#8221; &#8211; An individual who is under twenty-one (21) years of age.

&#8220;Recipient&#8221; &#8211; An individual who receives a subsidy under the Grandparent Caregivers Pilot Program.

&#8220;Resident of the District of Columbia&#8221; &#8211; A resident as defined by section 503 of the District of Columbia Public Assistance Act of 1982, effective April 6, 1982 (D.C. Law 4-101; D.C. Official Code § 4-205.03).

&#8220;Standby guardianship&#8221; &#8211; A legal status awarded by the court in accordance with D.C. Official Code § 16-4802 (2005 Supp.).

&#8220;Subsidy&#8221; &#8211; A subsidy provided under the Grandparent Caregivers Pilot Program.

&#8220;Subsidy agreement&#8221; &#8211; An agreement entered into between CFSA and a grandparent, great-grandparent, great-aunt or great-uncle concerning the provision of a subsidy under the Grandparent Caregivers Pilot Program.

&#8220;Substantiated&#8221; &#8211; Has the meaning set forth in the Improved Child Abuse Investigations Amendment Act of 2002, effective October 19, 2002, (D.C. Law 14-206; D.C. Official Code § 4-1301.02(19A)).

&#8220;Temporary Assistance for Needy Families&#8221; or &#8220;TANF&#8221; &#8211; the Temporary Assistance for Needy Families program established by section 201 of the District of Columbia Public Assistance Act of 1982, effective April 6, 1982 (D.C. Law 4-101; D.C. Official Code 4-202.01), which is administered through the Department of Human Services, Income Maintenance Administration.

SOURCE

Final Rulemaking published at 53 DCR 5837 (July 21, 2006); as amended by Final Rulemaking published at 55 DCR 12482 (December 12, 2008).
