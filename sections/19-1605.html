§ 19-1605. FILING AN OBJECTION (§ 19-1605)

1605.1 1605.1 The affected Advisory Neighborhood Commission or any resident or ownerf property within six hundred (600) feet of the boundary lines of the lot onhich the public hall is situated may file an objection to the issuance of a newr a renewal license. If an objection is filed, the Director shall hold aearing to make the determinations required by D.C. Official Code §7-2820(b-1)(2).

1605.2 1605.2 An objection shall be in writing and must be filed with theepartment’s Business and Professional Licensing Administration at least five5) calendar days prior to the date of the hearing. The objection shall list theame and address of the person making the objection and shall describe theeasons for the objection.

1605.3 1605.3 When a party retains counsel, the attorney so retained shall include hisr her name, address, phone number, and D.C. Bar number on the first documentiled by that party after retaining counsel. If the party retains counsel afteriling a document and the party does not file additional documents, the attorneyetained shall promptly file with the Department a notice of appearanceontaining the attorney’s name, address, phone number, and D.C. Bar number.

1605.4 1605.4 When a party is proceeding without representation by counsel, the partyhall include his or her name, address, and phone number on any document filedith the Department.
