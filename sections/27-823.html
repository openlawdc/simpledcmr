§ 27-823. SUBPOENAS (§ 27-823)

823.1 823.1 The Commission may upon its own motion, or upon the request of thepplicant or respondent, the Department, or the Assistant Attorney General ofecord, issue a subpoena requiring:

(a) The attendance and testimony of witnesses;

(b) The production of evidence, including, but not limited to books, records,orrespondence, or documents in the possession or under the control of theerson subpoenaed; and

(c) Access to evidence for the purpose of examination and copying.

823.2 823.2 The issuance and manner of service of a subpoena shall be as prescribedy the Civil Rules of the District of Columbia Superior Court.

823.3 823.3 The form of a subpoena may be prescribed by the Commission andubscribed by the Chairperson. A subpoena shall state the name and address ofts issuer; identify the evidence or person subpoenaed; the person to whom andhe place, date, and time at which it is returnable; the nature of the evidencehich is to be examined or copied; and the date and time when access isequested. A subpoena shall be returnable to the Chairperson of the Commission.

823.4 823.4 Upon failure of any person to comply with a subpoena issued under thisection, the Commission may seek enforcement of the subpoena through theistrict of Columbia Superior Court.
