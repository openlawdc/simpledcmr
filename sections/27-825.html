§ 27-825. EVIDENCE AT HEARINGS (§ 27-825)

825.1 825.1 In a proceeding before the Commission under this chapter, each partyhall have the right to:

(a) Present in person, or by counsel, that party’s case or defense,ncluding oral and documentary evidence;

(b) Submit rebuttal evidence; and

(c) Cross-examine opposing witnesses.

825.2 825.2 For documentary evidence to be admitted at a proceeding before theommission, it shall be submitted to the Commission at least five (5) days prioro the hearing, unless otherwise allowed by the Commission.

825.3 825.3 Testimonial evidence received at Commission hearings shall be under oathr affirmation.

825.4 825.4 The Commission shall exclude irrelevant, immaterial, or undulyepetitious evidence.

825.5 825.5 The Commission may take official notice of the following:

(a) The laws of the District of Columbia, the United States, and any state orurisdiction of the United States; or

(b) Any fact that is not subject to reasonable dispute because it is generallynown within the District of Columbia or is capable of accurate and readyetermination by resorting to reliable sources.

825.6 825.6 In an appeal of the Department’s decision to deny an applicant forertification, the burden of proof is on the applicant to establish that he orhe is eligible for certification by a preponderance of the evidence.

825.7 825.7 In an action to revoke the respondent’s certificate of registration,he burden of proof is on the Department to establish by a preponderance of thevidence the basis for the revocation.
