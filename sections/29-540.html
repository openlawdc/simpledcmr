§ 29-540. ADVANCED EMERGENCY MEDICAL SERVICES INSTRUCTOR STANDARDS (§ 29-540)

540.1 540.1 Advanced Emergency Medical Technician (AEMT) courses, Emergency Medicalechnician-Intermediate (EMT-I) courses, and Paramedic courses shall be taughty:

(a) District-certified Advanced Emergency Medical Services (AEMS) instructors;r

(b) AEMS instructor trainees under the supervision of a District-certified AEMSnstructor.

540.2 540.2 A candidate for AEMS instructor shall:

(a) Maintain a valid District Paramedic or greater certification or license, tonclude but not limited to Nurse or Physician;

Maintain a valid instructor certification in a related ALS discipline, such asdvanced Cardiac Life Support (ACLS) or Pediatric Advanced Life Support (PALS);

(c) Successfully complete a competency-based evaluation based on currentistrict policy; and

(d) Submit to the Director at the end of the interim teaching a completed AEMSnstructor application signed by the medical director verifying successfulvaluations and practice teaching dates.

540.3 540.3 Certification as an AEMS instructor shall not exceed two (2) years andhall be concurrent with the sponsoring EMS educational institution’sertification period.

540.4 540.4 In order to remain in good standing during the two (2) year period ofpproval an AEMS Instructor shall:

(a) Maintain current District EMS provider certification or license at thearamedic level or greater, to include but not limited to Nurse or Physician;

(b) Successfully complete a competency-based evaluation based on currentistrict policy;

(c) Attend AEMS instructor workshops as required; and

(d) Submit to the Director a completed AEMS instructor application signed by theedical director verifying successful evaluations and teaching dates.

540.5 540.5 If the educational institution’s medical director approves, the AEMSnstructor may also function as the AEMS program director.

540.6 540.6 An AEMS instructor whose certification has expired may apply to theirector for reinstatement within two (2) years of expiration.

540.7 540.7 An applicant for reinstatement as an AEMS instructor shall:

(a) Be certified or licensed as a District Paramedic or greater provider, tonclude but not limited to Nurse or Physician;

(b) Successfully complete a competency-based evaluation based on currentistrict policy; and

(c) Submit a completed AEMS instructor application showing successfulvaluations from practice teaching.

540.8 540.8 An AEMS instructor certification shall not exceed two (2) years and shalle concurrent with the sponsoring EMS educational institution’s certificationeriod.

540.9 540.9 An AEMS instructor whose certification has lapsed for more than two (2)ears shall be required to meet initial AEMS instructor requirements.
