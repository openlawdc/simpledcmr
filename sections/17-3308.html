§ 17-3308. REINSTATEMENT OF AN EXPIRED LICENSE (§ 17-3308)

3308.1. This section applies to applicants for reinstatement of an expired license issued under the following:

(a) An act listed on § 3300.1; or

(b) A law repealed or amended by an act listed in § 3300.1.

3308.2. An applicant for reinstatement under this section shall file an application with a board on the prescribed form and shall pay the required reinstatement fee.

3308.3. An applicant for reinstatement under this section shall demonstrate fitness to resume practice by submitting evidence satisfactory to a board that the applicant has the competency and knowledge of District and federal laws necessary to resume practice and that the applicant&#8217;s resumption of practice will not be detrimental to the public interest or the integrity of the occupation or profession.

3308.4. In making a determination pursuant to § 3308.3, a board shall consider the following:

(a) The length of time that the applicant had practiced in the District or another jurisdiction;

(b) The length of time after expiration of the applicant&#8217;s license that the applicant was not practicing either in the District or another jurisdiction;

(c) The applicant&#8217;s violations of any laws;

(d) The applicant&#8217;s present character; and

(e) The applicant&#8217;s present qualifications and competency to practice.

3308.5. A board may require an applicant to complete certain educational or training requirements, in addition to any continuing education requirements, prior to or after reinstatement, to ensure that the applicant is competent to practice.
