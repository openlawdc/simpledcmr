§ 24-2313. PERSONAL APPEARANCE AND FILING TIME (§ 24-2313)

2313.1 2313.1 In accordance with § 203 of the Act (D.C. Official Code § 7-2502.04(c)2013 Supp.)), each applicant for a registration certificate shall personallyresent the required form at the Firearms Registration Section, during operatingours.

2313.2 2313.2 Multiple applications submitted at one (1) time shall be accepted on theasis of a single personal appearance.

2313.3 2313.3 The Director may waive the requirement for a personal appearance inmergency situations, including cases where the applicant is out of the country,n the hospital, or not ambulatory; provided, that the application shall beccepted for processing, but shall not be approved until the applicant appearsn person.

2313.4 2313.4 If the condition preventing the personal appearance is permanent orontinuing in nature, the Director may, in his or her discretion, satisfy thisequirement by interviewing the applicant at a place convenient to thepplicant.

2313.5 2313.5 When a personal appearance is not made, an appropriate notation shall beade on the application showing that fact, together with the name, address,hone number, and relationship to the applicant of the person presenting thepplication on the person’s behalf.

2313.6 2313.6 A person other than the president or chief executive of an organizationay submit an application if that person presents with the application a lettern the organization’s official letterhead signed by the president or chiefxecutive of the organization, stating the name of the person appearing, thaterson’s position within the organization, and the identity of the weapon her she is authorized to present for registration.

2313.7 2313.7 When submitting an application, an applicant shall not have the firearmo be registered in his or her possession.

2313.8 2313.8 The Director may require an applicant to return with the firearm if itppears to the Director that any of the following conditions may apply:

(a) That the person is unqualified or incapable of safe and responsibleossession or use of the firearm;

(b) That the firearm may be unregisterable, defective, or in a dangerousondition or state of disrepair; or

(c) That the information relating to the weapon on the application isncorrect, misleading, or incomplete.

2313.9 2313.9 A person shall be deemed to be in compliance with the personalotification requirements of § 206(a) of the Act (D.C. Official Code §-2502.06(a) (2013 Supp.)) if he or she, immediately after bringing a firearmnto the District, telephonically notifies the Firearms Registration Section at02-727-4275.
