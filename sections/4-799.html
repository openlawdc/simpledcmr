§ 4-799. DEFINITIONS (§ 4-799)

799.1. When used in this chapter, the following terms and phrases shall have the meanings ascribed:

Aggrieved Party &#8211; any person claiming to be aggrieved by an unlawful discriminatory practice.

Act &#8211; the District of Columbia Human Rights Act of 1977, effective December 13, 1977 (D.C. Law 2-38; D.C. Official Code § 2-1401.01 et seq.).

Commission &#8211; the Commission on Human Rights, as established by section 401 of the Act (D.C. Official Code § 2-1404.01).

Complainant &#8211; any person or organization filing a complaint with the Office.

Complaint &#8211; a notarized statement filed with the Office that sets forth a claim of discrimination.

Day &#8211; a calendar day, unless otherwise specified.

Director &#8211; the Director of the Office, or a member of the Office staff specifically delegated the authority to act for the Director.

Office &#8211; the District of Columbia Office of Human Rights, as established by section 202 of the Act (D.C. Official Code § 2-1411.01).

Party or Parties &#8211; the complainant or the respondent.

Position Statement &#8211; the respondent’s official response to the allegations in the Charge of Discrimination.

Respondent &#8211; any person specified in the complaint as having engaged in an unlawful discriminatory practice.

SOURCE

Final Rulemaking published at 56 DCR 007324 (September 4, 2009).
