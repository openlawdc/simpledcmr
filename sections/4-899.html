§ 4-899. DEFINITIONS (§ 4-899)

899.1. When used in this chapter, the following terms and phrases shall have the meanings ascribed:

&#8220;Act&#8221; &#8211; the Human Rights Act of 1977, effective December 13, 1977 (D.C. Law 2-38; D.C. Official code § 2-1401.01 et seq.).

&#8220;Commission&#8221; &#8211; the Commission on Human Rights, established by section 401 of the Act (D.C. Official Code § 2-1404.01).

&#8220;Entities&#8221; &#8211; include all employers, housing providers, public accommodations, educational institutions, and government agencies and their contractors that come within the jurisdictional reach of the Act.

&#8220;Gender identity or expression&#8221; &#8211; a gender-related identity, appearance, expression, or behavior of an individual, regardless of the individual&#8217;s assigned sex at birth.

&#8220;Office&#8221; &#8211; the Office of Human Rights, established by section 202 of the Fiscal Year 2000 Service Improvement and Budget Support Act of 1999, effective October 20, 1999 (D.C. Law 13-38; D.C. Official Code § 2-1411.01).

&#8220;Transgender&#8221; &#8211; an adjective that refers to any individual whose identity or behavior differs from stereotypical or traditional gender expectations, including transsexual individuals, cross-dressers, androgynous individuals, and others whose appearance or characteristic are perceived to be gender-atypical.

SOURCE

Final Rulemaking published at 53 DCR 8751, 8756 (October 27, 2006).
