§ 17-3730. CONTINUING EDUCATION REQUIREMENTS FOR LICENSEES (§ 17-3730)

3730.1 3730.1 This section shall apply to all applicants for the renewal oreinstatement of a barber, cosmetology, or specialty cosmetology license,ncluding Manager and Instructor licenses, except those applicants seeking firstenewal of a license.

3730.2 3730.2 A continuing education credit shall be valid only if it is part of arogram or activity approved by the Board in accordance with § 3731. Licenseesre responsible for ensuring that continuing education courses taken to satisfyhe Board’s renewal or reinstatement requirements are Board certified orpproved.

3730.3 3730.3 An applicant for renewal of a license shall submit to the Board proof ofaving completed at least six (6) hours of credit in approved continuingducation programs during the term of the license. Two (2) of these hours shalle in health, safety, and welfare subjects. Four (4) of these hours shallonsist of general elective courses, as approved by the Board.

3730.4 3730.4 Licensees who also hold equivalent licenses in another jurisdiction may,t the Board’s discretion, substitute continuing education credits completedn the other jurisdiction for the general elective continuing educationequirements under this section if the licensee earned the continuing educationredits during the two (2) year period proceeding the date that the licensee’sistrict license expires.

3730.5 3730.5 The Board may approve educational offerings that include, but are notimited to, the following topics:

(a) Courses related to the practice of barbering, cosmetology, and specialtyosmetology;

(b) District laws and regulations;

(c) Ethics and standards of professional practice;

(d) Occupational health and safety;

(e) HIV/AIDS and communicable and infectious disease control;

(f) Product knowledge;

(g) Business;

(h) Management (Managers only); and

Education or Instruction (Instructors only).

3730.6 3730.6 Upon request by the Board, an applicant shall prove completion ofequired continuing education credits by submitting a certification ofompletion that includes the following:

(a) The name and address of the sponsor of the program;

(b) The name of the program, its location, a description of the subject matterovered, and the names of the instructors;

(c) The dates on which the applicant attended the program;

(d) The hours of credit claimed; and

(e) A verification of completion with the signature and seal of the sponsor.

3730.7 3730.7 An applicant for reinstatement of an expired license or foreinstatement of a suspended or revoked license shall submit proof pursuant to 3730.3 of having completed all continuing education credits that thepplicant would have been required to take per licensing cycle if thepplicant’s license had not expired or had not been suspended or revoked.

3730.8 3730.8 An applicant for the renewal of a license who fails to submit proof ofaving completed the continuing education requirements by or before thexpiration date may renew the license within sixty (60) days after expiration byubmitting proof pursuant to § 3730.6 and by paying the required late fee. Uponenewal, the Board shall deem the applicant to have possessed a valid licenseuring the period between the expiration of the license and its renewal.

3730.9 3730.9 If an applicant for the renewal of a license fails to submit proof ofompletion of continuing education requirements within sixty (60) days after thexpiration of the applicant’s license, the license shall be deemed to haveapsed on the date of expiration, and the applicant shall be required to applyor reinstatement of the expired license pursuant to § 3308 of this chapter.

3730.10 3730.10 The Board may grant an extension of the sixty (60) day period to renewfter expiration if the applicant’s failure to submit proof of completion wasor good cause. For purposes of this subsection, “good cause” includes prooff the following:

(a) Serious and protracted illness of the applicant who submits a doctor’statement verifying the illness;

(b) The death or serious and protracted illness of a member of thepplicant’s immediate family, which death or illness resulted in thepplicant’s inability to complete the continuing education requirements withinhe specified time. For the purposes of this subsection, the term “immediateamily” means the applicant’s spouse and any parent, brother, sister, orhild of the applicant and the spouse of any such parent, brother, sister, orhild;

(c) The applicant, due to age (seventy (70) years of age or older), is unableo complete the requirements within the specified time; or

(d) Active military service.
