§ 1-2973. PUBLIC BENEFITS CASES – ADMINISTRATIVE REVIEWS (§ 1-2973)

2973.1 2973.1 An administrative review is an informal meeting between the person whoas requested a hearing at OAH and a representative of the agency or servicerovider whose action or inaction is being challenged by that person. Theurpose of an administrative review is to determine whether the agency’s orervice provider’s position is valid and, if possible, to achieve an informalolution.

2973.2 2973.2 An agency or service provider shall offer each person who requests aearing at OAH an opportunity for an administrative review, if required by law.

At least five (5) calendar days before the hearing date, the agency or servicerovider shall file and send to all parties and their representatives a statuseport, which says whether an administrative review was held, and the results ofny review.

2973.3 2973.3 In cases involving shelter or other services for homeless persons, asequired by the Homeless Services Reform Act of 2005, D.C. Official Code §§-1601.01, et seq., the Department of Human Services shall conduct thedministrative review.

2973.4 2973.4 As required by law, the agency or service provider shall make the caseile available to the person who requested the hearing.
