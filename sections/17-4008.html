§ 17-4008. FALSE OR MISLEADING COMMUNICATIONS AND ADVERTISING (§ 17-4008)

4008.1. A health professional shall not make or cause to be made a false or misleading communication about the health professional or the health professional&#8217;s services.

4008.2. A health professional shall not falsely represent that the health professional is certified by, a member of, or otherwise endorsed by, a professional society, association, or other organization.

4008.3. A health professional shall not communicate the fact that the health professional practices a particular health occupation, or specializes in a particular field of the health occupation, unless the health professional is in fact a specialist in the particular field indicated or possesses any certificate required by the health profession to be a specialist in the field.

4008.4. Subject to this section, a health professional may advertise professional services through media, such as a telephone directory, legal directory, newspaper or other periodical, radio or television, or through written communication not involving personal contact.

4008.5. As used in this section, a communication is &#8220;false&#8221; or &#8220;misleading&#8221; if it:

(a) Contains a material misrepresentation or omits to make a representation necessary to make the statement considered as a whole not misleading; or

(b) Contains an assertion about the health professional or the health professional&#8217;s services that cannot be substantiated.
