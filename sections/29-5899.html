§ 29-5899. DEFINITIONS (§ 29-5899)

5899.1. When used in this chapter, the following terms and phrases shall have the meanings ascribed:

&#8220;Collateral Contact&#8221; &#8211; an oral confirmation, in-person or by telephone, of a person&#8217;s or household&#8217;s circumstances by a person outside of the household.

&#8220;DCPS&#8221; &#8211; the District of Columbia Public Schools.

&#8220;Department&#8221; &#8211; the Department of Human Services.

&#8220;DHS&#8221; &#8211; the District of Columbia Department of Human Services.

&#8220;Director&#8221; &#8211; the Director of the District of Columbia Department of Human Services.

&#8220;District&#8221; &#8211; agency of the government of the District of Columbia or any entity with which the government contracts for services.

&#8220;District of Columbia Public Assistance Act of 1982&#8243; &#8211; the District of Columbia Public Assistance Act of 1982, as amended, effective April 6, 1982 (D.C. Law 4-101; D.C. Official Code Section 4-201.1 et seq.)

&#8220;Full Family Sanction&#8221; &#8211; the termination of the entire cash assistance grant for a non-exempt TANF family.

&#8220;FY&#8221; &#8211; fiscal year.

&#8220;Good Cause&#8221; &#8211; a circumstance as defined in 29 DCMR § 5810.3.

&#8220;IMA&#8221; &#8211; the Income Maintenance Administration.

&#8220;Individual Responsibility Plan (IRP)&#8221; &#8211; a written agreement developed jointly by the customer and DHS or a DHS Provider that acts as the customer’s roadmap to securing employment and becoming self-sufficient. The IRP outlines specific steps that the customer agrees and commits to take in order to address and remove barriers, and find and retain employment.

&#8220;Individual Sanction&#8221; &#8211; a reduction in the Customer’s TANF grant whereby the needs of the work eligible household member is excluded from the assistance unit.

&#8220;Minor&#8221; &#8211; a person who has not attained eighteen (18) years of age.

&#8220;Participant&#8221; &#8211; a participant in a program which expends federal or D.C. funds to require or support work activities, including subsidized jobs, work experience, community service, and on-the-job training under TANF, Welfare-to-Work, and the Workforce Investment Act.

&#8220;POWER&#8221; &#8211; the Program on Work, Employment, and Responsibility.

&#8220;Pregnant or parenting teen&#8221; &#8211; a person who has a child or children, or is pregnant in the third trimester of the first pregnancy, and is under eighteen (18) years of age.

&#8220;Student&#8221; &#8211; is an individual who is attending a school, college, or university or a course of vocational or technical training designed to fit him or her for gainful employment and includes a participant in the Job Corps Program under the Job Training Partnership Act (JTPA), approved October 13, 1982 (Pub.L. 97-300, 29 U.S.C. Section 1501 et seq., as amended).

&#8220;TANF&#8221; &#8211; Temporary Assistance for Needy Families, as defined in the District of Columbia Public Assistance Act of 1982, as amended, effective April 6, 1982 (D.C. Law 4-101; D.C. Official Code Section 4-201.1 et seq.).

&#8220;TANF Service Provider&#8221; – The primary case manager service provider who is providing direct services to a TANF customer in accordance with the Customer’s Individual Responsibility Plan.

&#8220;Teen&#8221; &#8211; an individual who is less than twenty (20) years of age.

SOURCE

Final Rulemaking published at 48 DCR 10251 (November 9, 2001); as amended by Final Rulemaking published at 60 DCR 2863 (March 8, 2013).
