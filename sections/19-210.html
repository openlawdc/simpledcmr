§ 19-210. LEAVES OF ABSENCE (§ 19-210)

210.1 210.1 General Leave &#8211; A division director may grant a general leave ofbsence without pay to any regular full-time or regular part-time employee ofhe division. Any such leave beyond a period of seven (7) calendar days must bepproved by the President and Chief Executive Officer.

210.2 210.2 Family and Medical Leave &#8211; An employee of the Authority shall beligible to take the greater of twelve (12) weeks of unpaid leave during awelve month period, or sixteen (16) weeks of unpaid leave during a twenty-four24) month period, for family or medical purposes, in accordance with the D.C.amily and Medical Leave Act of 1990, D.C. Official Code §§ 32-501, et seq.2010 Repl.), and the Federal Family and Medical Leave Act of 1993, 29 U.S.C.§ 2601, et seq.

210.3 210.3 Jury Duty &#8211; Employees will receive their regular pay while servingn jury duty. An employee who is requested to report for jury duty shall informhe Court of this fact. The employee shall turn over to the President and Chiefxecutive Officer any compensation check other than for transportation.

210.4 210.4 Military Leave &#8211; The President and Chief Executive Officer shallrant an employee military leave of absence for a period not to exceed five (5)ears. Such an employee shall be restored to his or her former position if:

(a) The employee provided advance notice of the military service to theuthority, unless notice is excused by military necessity;

(b) The employee applies for reemployment within the time prescribed by law;

(c) The cumulative length of the employee&#8217;s absence and of all previousbsences from the Authority by reason of service in the military does not exceedive (5) years;

(d) The employee provides documentation of honorable discharge from militaryervice; and

(e) The employee is able to perform the duties of his or her former position.

210.5 210.5 Administrative Leave &#8211; In addition to other types of leave, theresident and Chief Executive Officer may grant administrative leave with payor reasons such as bereavement or hazardous weather.
