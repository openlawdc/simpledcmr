§ 30-308. AGENT RESPONSIBILITIES (§ 30-308)

308.1 308.1 Agents shall do the following:

(a) Provide for the sale of lottery tickets in accordance with instructionsssued by the Agency;

(b) Accept only cash or debit cards for the sale of lottery tickets.

Agents are not required to accept debit cards for the sale of lottery tickets,ut if an Agent chooses to accept debit cards, the Agent shall be responsibleor any costs, fees or charge backs that may be associated with debit cardottery sales.

(2) Agents may not charge a fee associated with accepting debit cards forottery ticket sales.

(c) Sell lottery tickets during all hours and days that the agent&#8217;sstablishment is open for business, at the location set forth in the license;

(d) Render services which meet the standards of the reasonable prudent businesserson;

(e) Be financially responsible to the Agency for all revenues derived from theale of lottery tickets and all tickets that are cashed or cancelled and areater presented to the Agency for payment;

(f) Be financially responsible to destroy all cashed lottery tickets and allancelled lottery tickets;

(g) Post prominently point-of-sale and other promotional materials supplied byhe Agency;

(h) Attend all training sessions;

(i) Provide a site for the location of any on-line computer terminal assignedo the agent;

(j) Provide, prior to the installation of the on-line computer terminal, aedicated electrical double duplex “U” grounded outlet on a separate circuithat remains on twenty-four (24) hours a day for the exclusive use of then-line computer terminal, which outlet shall be located within five feet (5t.) of the on-line computer terminal;

(k) Provide sufficient space for the proper operation of the on-line computererminal at the site approved by the Agency;

(l) Exercise due diligence in the operation and care of the on-line computererminal and immediately notify the Agency&#8217;s computer operation center ofny on-line computer terminal malfunction;

(m) Permit a representative of the Agency to conduct on-site inspections ofottery property, including the on-line computer terminal, during thegent&#8217;s normal hours of operation;

(n) Provide for the cashing of all lottery tickets and pay all claims up to aaximum of six hundred dollars ($600.00), or such other amount as may beetermined by the Executive Director, in cash, regardless of where the winningottery ticket was purchased;

(o) Cash checks for lottery winnings without charging fees;

(p) Ascertain the winning numbers and immediately post them in a prominentlace after each drawing;

(q) Replace ribbons and ticket stock as required in the on-line computererminal;

(r) Perform no mechanical or electrical maintenance on the on-line computererminal;

(s) Notify the Agency in writing at least fourteen (14) days in advance of thegent&#8217;s intent to temporarily cease operations (e.g., vacation), or asoon as possible in the case of an emergency;

(t) Notify the Agency in writing of a change of address of the location, typef establishment or ownership of the establishment, in accordance with § 206 ofhis title;

(u) Notify the Agency in writing seven (7) days in advance of a change of bankccount maintained for the deposit of on-line revenues pursuant to § 305.1.

(v) Pay the cost for all telephone lines resulting from agent relocations;

(w) Permit no persons under eighteen (18) years of age to sell lottery tickets;

(x) Provide a certificate of insurance, which indicates that the agent has andaintains casualty insurance, approved by the Agency, to cover the replacementost of the on-line computer terminal and other Agency property assigned to thegent. The agent shall notify the Agency in writing of any change in insuranceoverage fifteen (15) days in advance of the change; and
