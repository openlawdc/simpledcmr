§ 27-834. AGENCY SET-ASIDE AND PREFERENCE PROGRAM IMPLEMENTATION (§ 27-834)

834.1 834.1 When an entire solicitation has been placed in the SBE set-asiderogram, the solicitation shall:

(a) State that it is a set-aside for certified small business enterprisefferors under the provisions of the Act; and

(b) Require that responses include a copy of the SBE’s letter ofertification.

834.2 834.2 Once a solicitation has been placed in the SBE set-aside market asequired by sections 2344 and 2345 of the Act, the agency shall not remove itrom the set-aside market unless:

(a) At least one attempt to solicit bids or proposals for the procurement inhe SBE set-aside market has failed to produce a responsive bid or proposal;

(b) There is no SBE that qualifies for the procurement;

(c) The prices of the bids or proposals are 12% or more above the likely pricen the open market; or

(d) The removal is authorized by law.

834.3 834.3 Each agency shall provide to the Department, if requested, the followingypes of procurement records:

(a) Small purchase sources;

(b) Term contracts;

(c) Blanket purchases orders;

(d) Repetitive or recurring procurement; and

(e) GSA Schedule procurement.

834.4 834.4 If an agency with independent contracting authority or the Office ofontracting and Procurement (“OCP”) intends to place a solicitation on thepen market for a contract or procurement of $100,000 or less, the agency or OCPhall provide a written notice to the Department as soon as practicable that thegency intends to exclude the contract or procurement from its set-asiderogram. The written notice shall include, at a minimum, a description of:

(a) The steps taken to identify SBEs that may be able to provide the goods orervices; and

(b) The information contained in the written determination(s) required underection 2344 or 2345 of the Act.
