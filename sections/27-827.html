§ 27-827. REOPENING AND RECONSIDERATION (§ 27-827)

827.1 827.1 An applicant or respondent may file a motion for reconsideration withhe Commission, based on new evidence, misapplication of law, or misstatement of material fact within ten (10) days after the date on which the applicant orespondent was served with the Commission&#8217;s decision.

827.2 827.2 The motion for reconsideration shall be in writing and shall state theollowing:

(a) The matters of record alleged to have been erroneously decided;

(b) The grounds relied upon; and

(c) The relief sought.

827.3 827.3 If a motion under this section is based wholly or partially on newvidence, the new evidence or arguments shall be set forth in an affidavit whicherifies that the petitioner could not with due diligence have become aware ofhe new evidence prior to the Commission hearing.

827.4 827.4 The government may file a response to a motion to reconsider and theommission may decide a motion to reconsider without a hearing.

827.5 827.5 The Commission may reopen a hearing where an applicant or respondent hasailed to appear at a scheduled hearing, provided that the applicant orespondent submits a motion in writing setting forth good cause for thepplicant’s or respondent&#8217;s failure to appear. The failure to report ahange of address pursuant to this chapter does not constitute good cause.

827.6 827.6 A motion to reopen a hearing shall be filed within ten (10) days of theate of the hearing.

827.7 827.7 Neither the submission nor the granting of a motion under this sectionhall operate as a stay of a decision unless so ordered by the Commission.

827.8 827.8 The failure of the Commission to issue an order in response to a motionnder this section within thirty (30) days of the submission of the motion shallonstitute a denial of the motion.
